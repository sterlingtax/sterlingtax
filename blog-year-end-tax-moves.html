Four year-end moves that can lower your tax bill

November 20264 min read

Most of what decides your April tax bill is already locked in by the time you file. The good news is that the last few weeks of the year are when you still have room to act. Here are four moves worth a look before December 31.

1. Top off your retirement accounts

Adding to a 401(k) or IRA lowers your taxable income for the year, and it quietly builds your future at the same time. If you have room left in your contribution limit, the end of the year is the time to use it. A quick check now can tell you how much room you have.

2. Take your required distributions

If you are at the age where the IRS requires withdrawals from certain retirement accounts, missing the deadline comes with a steep penalty. It is an easy thing to overlook and an easy thing to get right with a reminder.

3. Be intentional about gains and losses

If you sold investments at a gain this year, selling others that are down can offset some of that gain. It will not make sense in every account or every situation, so it is worth a short conversation before you act.

4. Give on purpose, not just in December

Charitable giving can lower your taxes, but only if it is done in a way that actually counts on your return. Grouping a few years of giving into one, or giving appreciated stock instead of cash, can go further than writing a check. A little planning makes the same generosity work harder.

The real point

None of these are complicated on their own. The trick is looking at them together, and doing it while there is still time on the clock. That is exactly what a year-end check is for. If you want a second set of eyes before the year closes, we are happy to help.
